MIT CFC  -  CAS LREIS
Joint Program

 

MIT China Future City Lab is going to collaborate with State Key Laboratory of Resources and Environmental Information System at Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS LREIS). The objective is to foster students, expand interdisciplinary research and develop advance techniques in the fileds of urban environment, geographic information, spatial big data, smart city and related subjects.

The MIT China Future City Lab is a research and entrepreneurship program that focuses on the challenges of urban development in China, hosted at the MIT Department of Urban Studies and Planning.

Resources and Environmental Information System at Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS LREIS) is founded in 1985, one of the ealiest state key laboratories in China. It is the cradle for pioneers who establish the Chinese geographic information system.

With great research foundation, we can help each other in the fields of innovative urban research on the basis of spatial and temporal data analytics. With preliminary agreement, we are planning to form CAS LREIS - MIT CFC Center for Urban Science Research. 


The center will be led by a management commitee, formed by representatives from both labs. The center will have two directors, one from MIT and the other one from CAS. Directors hold the responsibility of the lab. 

Collaboration Pillar

To promote the cooperation between CAS LREIS and MIT CFC, utilizing advantages of each research labs, and strengthen the collaboration regarding scientifc research, academic visits, application projects, and student programs, both parties agree on cooperation intentions as follows:

Scientific Research

- Engage smart city research based on big data technologies.
- Enhancing the application of advanced methods, such as GIS, RS, IoT, to urban studies.
- Encouraging researchers from both labs to publish high-quality research papers.

Academic Visits

- Short-term academic visit program at MIT for CAS researchers.
- Priority visiting scholar program at MIT for CAS researchers.
- Academic conferences and workshops by CAS & MIT.

Application Projects

- Co-applying China-US cooperation programs of National Natual Science Foundation of China.
- Co-applying intermational cooperation programs in the US.
- Enhancing the application and commercialization of our research.

Student Programs

- Sponsoring visiting programs between MIT and CAS.
- Encouraging graduate students in CAS to apply for MIT.
- Co-supervising graduate students by CAS & MIT faculties.

Research Themes

As a complex system, the internal interactions between human activities and economic impact in the cities are the focues area of research. Based on the characteristics of both labs, the research themes of CAS LREIS - MIT CFC Center for Urban Science Research include:

Basic Theoretical Research

Explore urban science, including the construction of basic theories and methodologies of urban science, and the formation of urban scientific research methods and paradigms supported by new technologies.

Sensing and Analyzing Cities

Using mobile Internet, Internet of Things, sensor network and other sensing technologies to acquire, calculate, analyze and express the spatial structure, human activities and social and economic development of cities, based on urban data science and geographic information system.

Cross-cutting Research 

Promote integration of multiple disciplines (including geography, economics, urban science, computer science, etc.), and conduct innovative research in the areas of urban environmental policy and governance, climate change, urban networks, and computational social sciences.

Urban Technology Industry

Future City Innovation Connector, pilot case study from FCIC cohort startups to examine how digital technologies facilitate innovation and enables transformation in the real estate industries.

Form and scale of cooperation

Researchers at the CAS LREIS - MIT CFC Urban Science Research Center conduct joint research projects. At the beginning of the collaboration, the composition of laboratory staff will be 2-3 research directors at the MIT, 3-5 research directors from LREIS, and 10 - 15 other researchers (including associate researchers, postdoctoral fellows, research assistants, etc.)

Student opportunity

Graduate students exchange programs, post-doctoral programs, and joint training graduate students. Joint graduates or postdoctoral students are recruited by MIT, and the Chinese Academy of Sciences will recommend applicants. Joint training of doctoral students or postdocs requires two years of work in the CAS LREIS - MIT CFC center.

Cooperation and communication

The two sides accept each other's academic visits. exchanges. and research cooperations. The CAS LREIS - MIT CFC Center will host several academic seminars each year to provide a platform for discussions among professors, graduate students, researchers, management and technical staff from MIT and CAS.

The Chinese side will regularly select researchers from the State Key Laboratory to visit MIT and invit famous MIT scientists to visit Chinese laboratories.
